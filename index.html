RESEARCH

RESEARCH ASSISSTANT, KOREN LAB (JAN 2019 - PRESENT)

CORNELL UNIVERSITY

The Koren lab focuses on developing new experimental and computational approaches for measuring replication timing across the entire genome. My work focuses on the effects of 3D chromatin structure on the evolution of DNA replication timing in primates. I utilize a variety of assays, including ChIP-Seq, ATAC-Seq, and Hi-C, for humans, chimpanzees, and macaques to approximate each species’ chromatin structure. This information is combined with DNA replication timing profiles for each species to construct analysis pipelines.

RESEARCH ASSISSTANT, JAFFREY LAB (JUN 2021 - AUG 2021)

MEMORIAL SLOAN KETTERING CANCER CENTER & WEIL CORNELL MEDICINE

The Jaffrey lab focuses on uncovering how protein expression is regulated in normal compared to diseased cells. A vital tool for this research is paired CRISPRa/i screens, which activate or inhibit multiple genes at once. Since they are time-intensive and can be difficult to set up, I used existing RNA-seq and CRISPR knockout datasets to simulate these screens computationally. Combined with gene ontology analyses, the results reproduced findings from published in vitro screens on genes such as ADAR1 and PARP14.

DATA SCIENCE INTERN (JUN 2017 - AUG 2018)

SENSCIO SYSTEMS

At Senscio Systems, I worked on the clinical trial of their platform that aims to use Artificial Intelligence to monitor chronically ill patients from home. I processed electronic health records (EHRs) of over 200 patients to determine the cause of their hospital visits before and during the trial. I also combined patients’ usage of the platform with information from EHRs to examine patterns in individuals’ interactions with the platform leading up to hospital visits. These informed care teams of signs that a patient may need intervention to prevent hospitalization, resulting in tangible impacts on the quality and outcomes of patient care

PROJECTS & PUBLICATIONS

Predicting Alzheimer's risk of progression using machine learning

This project aims to understand which Alzheimer’s patients to prioritize for treatment with the newly released drug Aducanumab. To achieve this, we are utilizing ADNI data to predict patients' risk of progression. Since medical tests have varying costs, we also aim to determine the most cost-effective set of tests for doctors to accurately assess this risk.

ADNI Database

Effects of 3D chromatin structure on evolution of DNA replication

Replication timing differs even among humans and is closely linked to genetic diseases such as cancer. Discovering the mechanisms that cause timing changes is therefore key in understanding these diseases. My work focuses on the effects of 3D chromatin structure on the evolution of DNA replication timing in primates.

The Koren Lab

Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe AI

Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe is a strategic 2-player game, that consists of a 3x3 grid, with each square containing another Tic-Tac-Toe game. To win the game, players must win three of these smaller games in a row. We are creating an AI bot for players to play against using approaches such as Minimax Search and Monte Carlo Tree Search.

‍Impact of event severity on hospital rankings for heart failure readmission

While working on Senscio System's clinical trial, we noticed that their hospitalizations could vary drastically in severity (i.e. ranging from a short emergency room visit to being transferred to the ICU) and wondered if these should all be treated equally in our clinical trial analysis, which led to this project. The results suggested that giving more weight to severe readmission might provide additional information on hospital quality of care and may change how hospitals are ranked in the Medicare Program.

Paper (Population Health Management)

Health economic evaluation of an ultrathin eluting coronary stent

The BIOFLOW V trial compared an ultrathin, bioresorbable polymer sirolimus-eluting stent to a thin, durable polymer everolimus-eluting stent. The ultrathin stent had a significantly lower rate of peri-procedural myocardial infarction. This analysis simulated mortality and cost impact of that difference from a U.S. health system perspective over 12 months, which was estimated to translate into cost reductions of $124 per patient in 2018 US$.

Paper (Cardiovascular Revascularization Medicine)
